Project Introduction :
Sharing videos online is a huge part of how we connect today. Sites like Facebook, YouTube and Netflix let us watch anything anytime through streaming. But as more users stream together simultaneously, and expect faster speeds, building systems that can handle massive demand is no small feat.

This project aimed to develop a next-generation solution for streaming through innovative designs, tricks, and network tweaks. The main goal was lightning-fast loading regardless of how many viewers with minimum server/hardware cost. To pull this off, a super distributed and modular backend needs to be crafted for efficient conversion and storage of files in the server.

Encoding will be tweaked to the max to minimize prep times by splitting work into tiny pieces and passing between steps smoothly. Clever caching, load balancing, and internet protocols then shipped raw files seamlessly to end users lighting fast. Adaptive options tailor quality dynamically depending on bandwidth, keeping playback smooth even on bad/normal connections.

Compression methods should go through meticulous benchmarking to find the best encoding for each content format and resolution. Throughput, CPU usage, visuals, and compression ratios were exhaustively profiled to pick the most effective codecs. Storage platforms likewise saw extensive prototyping to arrive at a scalable organization maximizing retrieval pace for uninterrupted serving.

By tackling these core technical hurdles through pioneering systems design and algorithmic optimizations, the solution should be able to achieve unparallel speed, reliability and elasticity.
